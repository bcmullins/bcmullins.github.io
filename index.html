About Me

I am an incoming Assistant Professor of Computer and Information Science at Allegheny College working on trustworthy computing and statistical data privacy. I completed my PhD in computer science at UMass Amherst, where I worked with Dan Sheldon and Gerome Miklau, and was a Visiting Professor at Mount Holyoke College.

Before this, I was a Data Scientist in industry and a researcher at the Fiscal Research Center, a public policy think tank in Atlanta. I studied mathematics & logic at Miami University and philosophy & economics at Georgia State University.

Click here to view my CV. You may contact me at brettcmullins(at)gmail.

This radio program on Frank Ramsey influenced my thinking and interests greatly.

Research Interests

Differential Privacy, Explainable Machine Learning, Synthetic Data,
Learning Theory, Causation, Formal Epistemology

My research involves using tools from mathematics, logic, and machine learning to approach problems from a novel perspective.
